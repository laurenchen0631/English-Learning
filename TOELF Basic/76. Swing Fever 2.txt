[Real Article]
The New York Times suggested that the craze was getting out of hand, quoting a psychologist on the "dangerous hypnotic influence of swing, cleverly devised to a faster tempo than seventy-two bars to the minute - faster than the human pulse." 
Actually, swing was nothing more than jazz under a new name. Black groups in the 1920's had developed jazz, which was characterized by a driving rhythm and improvised solos; but few Whites heard anything except mild orchestrated versions of it. Then in 1935, a young, 24-year-old named Benny Goodman swept the nation with the swinging sound, lifting jazz into the mainstream of American culture.
