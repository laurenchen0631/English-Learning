[Real Article]
Between 1900 and 1910 some nine million immigrants came to America, drawn by the conviction that in this land of opportunity they would find what the old country had denied them. What they found in America severely tested their faith - and their courage and stamina as well. Most newcomers were doomed to years of toil (12 hours a day and six to seven days a week), at low pay (an average of less than $12.50 a week) in garment-making sweatshops, coal mines, and dirty factories. And though they were welcomed by Americans of good will, many encountered ridicule and prejudice. Disillusioned by bigotry and poverty, a large number of immigrants gave up and returned home; 395,000 departed in 1908 alone. But majority persevered.
