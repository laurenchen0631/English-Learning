[Real Article]
In 1938, at New York City's Paramount Theater, a band on an elevated platform rose out of the orchestra pit as 3,000 teenagers screamed and, as the musicians began to play, danced in the aisles. The band was Benny Goodman's, and the music he was playing was "swing." In the closing years of the decade, all of America was swept by swing fever. In little towns girls in loose skirts and saddle shoes filled ice-cream parlors with their boyfriends, dropped nickels into jukeboxes, and danced their favorite dance step, the jitterbug. Enthusiastic fans clustered around their radios to hear their favorite bands on weekly program and on Saturdays they tuned on Your Hit Parade to learn the top 10 hits of the week.
